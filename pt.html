Top Tourist Destinations of Pattanamthitta

More than fifty per cent of the total area of this district is covered by forests. Pathanamthitta is one of the most important pilgrim centers in Kerala and there are many Temples and Churchs. Maramon, the place where the world famous Maramon convention is conducted, is in this district. Sabarimala,located at an altitude of 3790 feet, is one of the most famous pilgrim centers in India. The world famous Aranmula mirrors comes from this district. The elephant-training center at Konni is also another attraction of the district.

konni EcoTourism

Konni, famous for the elephant training happening in the state attracts a huge number of visitors every year. People visit here to see the biggest animal on land in close quarters without fear. One of the major attractions at Konni is the giant wooden cages or the elephant kraals used to house the elephants.

Aruvikkal Waterfalls

Aruvikkal waterfall is a beautiful waterfall which is situated in Pampakuda, in the middle of the towns of Mvatupuzha and Piravom in Ernakulam district of Kerala, India. Pampakuda is a village surrounded by scenic beauty. This is an apt spot to enjoy with family for a day. The best time to visit this waterfall is from june to july during the monsoon. The nearest railway station is Piravom Vellor,15km and the nearest airport is Nedumbassery,40km

Gavi EcoTourism

Gavi is an Eco-Tourism a project of the Kerala Forest Development Corporation located in Pathanamthitta district, Kerala. Gavi, located in Pathanamthitta district offers its visitors activities like trekking, wildlife watching, outdoor camping in specially built tents, and night safaris.

Perunthenaruvi Waterfalls

Situated in the forest region of Sabarimala in Pathanamthitta district, one of the major pilgrim centres of South India, Perunthenaruvi allures tourists with its breathtakingly beautiful waterfalls and the dazzling stream that flows to merge with the sacred Pamba River. The locality is the home turf of beehives which have bestowed the place with the title Perunthenaruvi meaning the “River of Honey”.

Sabarimala

The temple is situated on a hilltop (about 3000 feet above sea level) named Sabarimala in Pathanamthitta district, which is unique. The temple is open to people belonging to all religions. There is a place near the temple; east of Sannidhanam (the abode of Lord Ayyappa), dedicated to Vavar (a close friend of Lord Ayyappa) which is called Vavaru Nada, an epitome of religious harmony. Another unique aspect of this temple is that it is not open throughout the year. It is open for worship only during the days of Mandalapooja, Makaravilakku, Vishu and also during the first day of every Malayalam month.

Mavarapara

Mavara Para in Kurampala South, Pathanamthitta is a top player in the category Tourist Attraction in the Pathanamthitta.Mavarapara boasts of a huge rock structure created after quarrying for granite.
