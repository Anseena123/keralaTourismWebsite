Kerala, located on the south-western tip of India, enjoys unique geographical features that have made it one of the most sought-after tourist destinations in Asia. Fondly referred to as ‘God’s Own Country’, Kerala was selected by the National Geographic Traveller as one of the 50 destinations of a lifetime and one of the thirteen paradises in the world.

An equable climate, serene beaches, tranquil stretches of backwaters, lush hill stations and exotic wildlife are the major attractions of this land. A unique advantage of Kerala is that most of the destinations here are only a two - four hour drive from the other.

Classical art forms, colourful festivals, exotic cuisine are some of the cultural marvels that await travellers. Ayurveda, the ancient Indian system of medicine and Panchakarma, the rejuvenation therapy in Ayurveda have also helped Kerala to gain a pan-global reputation as a worth-visit destination.

Season never ends in Kerala, thanks to the year-long moderate climate and numerous festivals and events.

Kerala Beach Tourism

Kerala, situated on the Malabar Coast of India, is home to some of the most stunning beaches in the country. From the sprawling palm-fringed shores of Kovalam to the secluded but stunning beaches of Varkala, Kerala is a paradise for beach lovers. Kovalam beach is one of the most popular beaches in Kerala. Located just 16 km from Thiruvananthapuram, Kovalam is a paradise for beach lovers. The beach is known for its three adjacent crescent-shaped beaches. It is also one of the few beaches in the world where you can get a view of the sunset and sunrise at the same time. There are several beach activities here like beach volleyball, swimming, sunbathing and catamaran rides. Varkala beach is another great beach in Kerala. Located in the Thiruvananthapuram district, Varkala is known for its stunning views of the Arabian Sea. It is also known for its cliff overlooking the beach which provides an excellent view of the sunset. The beach is known for its tranquility and is a great place for swimming, sunbathing and relaxing. Kappad beach is another great beach in Kerala.

Kerala Cultural Tourism

Kerala is a state in southern India that is known for its culture and its natural beauty. Kerala is known as the "God's Own Country" because of its unique culture and traditions. Kerala is home to some of the most beautiful natural landscapes, exotic wildlife, and vibrant festivals. It is also known for its Ayurvedic treatments, backwaters, and traditional art forms such as Kathakali and Mohiniattam. Kerala has become one of the most popular tourist destinations in India due to its cultural heritage and stunning scenery. Kerala Cultural Tourism offers a range of activities to explore the culture of the state. Visitors can explore the backwaters of Kerala, take part in temple festivals, visit the many museums and art galleries, or explore the performing arts such as Kathakali and Mohiniattam. The state also offers a range of cultural experiences such as Ayurvedic treatments, yoga and meditation, and traditional arts and crafts. There are also various cultural festivals celebrated throughout the year, from the Onam festival in August to the Thrissur Pooram in April. Kerala Cultural Tourism also provides visitors with an opportunity to explore the local cuisine.

Kerala Farm Tourism

Kerala is known for its lush greenery and beautiful landscapes. The state is home to some of the most beautiful farms in India. Kerala farm tourism offers an experience unlike any other. Visitors can enjoy the scenic beauty of the state while also exploring the various agricultural activities and the local culture. From the tranquil backwaters to the aromatic spice gardens and the vibrant tea plantations, Kerala has something for everyone. Kerala's farm tourism offers a unique opportunity to experience the traditional rural life of the state. Visitors can get up close to nature and see firsthand the different farming activities that are carried out in the region. From harvesting and ploughing to threshing and winnowing, visitors can gain insight into the intricacies of traditional farming methods. Visitors can also learn about the various herbs, spices, and vegetables that are grown in the region. Many farms in Kerala have started offering visitors a farm-to-table experience and the chance to pick their own vegetables. Kerala is also home to some of the most spectacular tea plantations in the world. The state's tea gardens offer visitors a fascinating glimpse into the world of tea production.
